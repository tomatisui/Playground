## M3 Choice Count Policy

This note freezes the visible choice-count policy for the working-memory family prototype.

## Active policy

- Visible choices for `M3` = `6`
- Visible choices for `M3-R` = `6`
- Maximum sequence length = `5`

## Why visible choices are 6

The task should be able to probe working memory up to a 5-item span while still preserving at least one distractor choice on screen.

If the visible choice count were lower:

- the max span would crowd out distractors
- the task would feel less like a real ordered selection task
- the sequence-selection interface would drift away from the intended screening logic

## Why max sequence length remains 5

- A 5-item span is the longest current prototype target
- With 6 visible choices, a 5-item target still leaves exactly 1 distractor
- This keeps the task challenging without collapsing into a pure recall-without-choice task

## How the 7-word familiarization pool relates to 6 visible choices

- The pool contains `7` familiar nouns for learning and later item composition
- Any single practice or test screen shows `6` visible choices
- This means one pool item can stay out of view on a given trial
- At the maximum span of `5`, the design still preserves `1` distractor among the visible choices

## Internal interpretation

This is a prototype UI/task-logic policy only. It is not a normed or diagnostic rule.
